Rich Dad Poor Dad

Robert T. Kiyosaki with Sharon Lechter (1997)

Sinopse

The book presents the perspective of the author, Robert Kiyosaki, who had two fathers: his "Poor Dad" (his highly educated biological father, who was financially struggling) and his "Rich Dad" (his friend's father, a successful entrepreneur who lacked formal education). The book advocates for the importance of financial literacy, financial independence, and building assets that generate passive income, rather than relying solely on a traditional job and salary. Kiyosaki emphasizes the need to understand how money works, how to invest, and the difference between an asset (something that puts money in your pocket) and a liability (something that takes money out of your pocket). It challenges conventional thinking about career and finance, encouraging readers to start their own businesses and invest in real estate or other income-generating ventures.
